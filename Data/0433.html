Hi Bob,

My impulse is to agree with Jonathan Gregory -- that in the conversion
to CF the auxiliary coordinate variables must become explicit and share
the dimension sizes with the measured variables. Could your conversion
program perform this calculation? The down side is that the coordinate
variables may plump up in size to exceed the measured variable(s).

The issue highlights the compromises that occur when a single format is
conceived for both archival and interchange. A solution in this case (a
thought exercise) might be to build an OPeNDAP server that would read
the HDF-EOS files (with in-built compression mechanisms such as the one
under discussion for efficient archival) and would output a CF-compliant
DAP representation (that can be read by the standard netCDF client API).

    - steve

> The increment and offset are scalars. In HDF-EOS, by convention
> either both of them are positive (meaning a small geolocation
> dimension -> larger data dimension) or both negative (large
> geolocation -> smaller data - I think they threw that one in for
> generality, not that they ever expect to see it implemented).

>
> Geoloc[0] has the value for Data[1] (0 * 2 + 1)

> etc. ...

> consider some sort of curve fit over more points.

> common, and the HDF-EOS API supports N-dimensions. My head hurts...
